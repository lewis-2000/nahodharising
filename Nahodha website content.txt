ORGANIZATION IDENTITY
What is Nahodha Rising?
Nahodha Rising is a community-based organization focused on mentoring, empowering, and transforming boys and girls through education, life skills, mentorship, and character development.
Why was Nahodha Rising started?
It was started to address the growing challenges facing children, especially boys, including lack of mentorship, poor role models, drug abuse, and school dropout.
What problem are you trying to solve?
The organization seeks to solve the crisis of neglected child development, indiscipline, substance abuse, lack of mentorship, and limited educational opportunities, primarily boy child.
What inspired the organization?
The desire to raise a responsible generation of young people equipped to positively influence society.
What does Nahodha Rising stand for?
Hope, transformation, leadership, discipline, empowerment, and restoration of purpose among children and youth.
What values define the movement?
Integrity, equality, inclusion, discipline, responsibility, empowerment, and respect.
What scripture guides the mission?
Proverbs 22:6 – “Train up a child in the way he should go...”
1 Timothy 4:12 – “Set an example for the believers…”
What does success look like?
Seeing children excel academically, develop strong character, avoid harmful behaviors, and become leaders in their communities.
What kind of men do you hope boys become?
Responsible, disciplined, God-fearing, respectful, confident, and productive leaders.

FOUNDING STORY
Who started Nahodha Rising?
It was started by passionate community leaders and mentors burdened by the challenges affecting boys and vulnerable children.
When was it started?
(2023)
What led to its creation?
The increasing cases of substance abuse, school dropout, broken mentorship systems, and lack of positive male guidance.
Specific realization?
The realization that many boys lacked direction and were vulnerable to negative influences.
Challenges noticed?
Poor discipline, peer pressure, hopelessness, low self-esteem, and lack of mentorship.

CHALLENGES BOYS FACE
What struggles are boys facing today?
Drug abuse, peer pressure, broken families, school dropout, identity crisis, and lack of role models.
What are you seeing in communities?
Rising indiscipline, hopelessness, violence, and emotional struggles among young boys.
Why is mentorship important?
It provides guidance, accountability, emotional support, and direction.
What happens without guidance?
Many boys fall into destructive habits, poor decision-making, and unfulfilled potential.
Harmful environments?
Substance abuse, negative peer influence, neglect, and lack of parental support.
Existing gaps?
Limited structured mentorship programs and insufficient community support systems.
